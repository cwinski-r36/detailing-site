3 Business Email & iPhone Setup

For Now — Free Gmail Account

Start with a free Gmail address: flatirondetail@gmail.com (or similar — check availability when creating it). It's free, works great on iPhone, and is perfectly fine for a new business. Once the business is making money, upgrade to a custom domain email like noah@flatirondetail.com using Google Workspace (~$6/month).

Check Availability First Go to gmail.com and try to create the account — popular names get taken. Good options to try: flatirondetail@gmail.com, flatirondetailco@gmail.com, flatirondetailboulder@gmail.com

Setup Steps

  1. Go to gmail.com → Create account → choose a business-sounding username
  2. Complete the signup (use a real recovery phone number)
  3. Update the email address on the website Contact page to the new Gmail

Adding to iPhone

  1. Open Settings → Mail → Accounts → Add Account
  2. Tap Google
  3. Sign in with the new Gmail address and password
  4. Toggle Mail on → Save

Done — shows up in the iPhone Mail app immediately. Both Noah and Halen can sign in on their own phones so they both see incoming messages.

Future Upgrade Once you have a domain name and the business is earning, switch to Google Workspace (~$6/month) for a professional @flatirondetail.com address. The setup takes about 30 minutes and everything migrates easily.
4 Square Appointments — Online Booking

What It Does

Customers visit the site, tap "Book Now," pick a service, pick a date and time, and book themselves. Noah and Halen get a push notification and it appears in their calendar automatically. No back-and-forth texting to schedule.

Cost

Square Appointments is free for small teams. You only pay the standard Square processing fee (2.6% + 10¢) if you collect payment at booking — which you can skip entirely and collect in person instead.

Part A — Create the Square Account

  1. Go to squareup.com/appointments and sign up (free)
  2. Download the Square Appointments app on both iPhones
  3. In the app: Services → add each service with name, duration, and price
  4. Set your availability — the days and hours you're willing to take bookings
  5. Add both Noah and Halen as team members so the calendar is shared between them

Part B — Connect to the Website

  1. In Square dashboard → Online Booking → copy your booking link
  2. The "Book Now" buttons on the website get updated to point to that link — no coding needed, just a URL swap
  3. Optional: Square also provides an embeddable widget that can sit directly on the Contact page

Part C — Day-to-Day Management

  1. New bookings trigger a push notification on their iPhones via the Square app
  2. View and manage the calendar from the app — accept, reschedule, or cancel bookings
  3. Customers automatically receive a confirmation email when they book
  4. Square sends automatic 24-hour reminders to customers (reduces no-shows)
  5. Block off unavailable times directly in the app (school days, vacations, etc.)
Pro Tip Set a minimum booking lead time of 24 hours so they're never getting same-day bookings they can't handle.
